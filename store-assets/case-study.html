How It Works

02

Just describe what you half-remember

Retrace runs quietly in the background, building a private index of every page you visit. When you want to find something, open a new tab and ask — the same way you'd describe it to a friend. No exact title needed. No URL. Just what you remember.

Every content type on a page — text, images, audio, video, docs — unified into a single searchable index.

Example — finding a half-remembered article

›

What was that article about why we remember some dreams and not others?

Privacy

04

Your history stays yours

Retrace knows a lot about you — by design. That's the whole point. But that information never goes anywhere. There is no Retrace server. No account. Your browsing history never leaves your machine.

What "local-first" actually means

  • ◆ Your index. Stored in your browser's local database — never on a server.
  • ◆ Your API key. Encrypted local storage only. The developer never sees it.
  • ◆ Your queries. Search happens locally first. Only the question text reaches the AI — not your history.
  • ◆ Your control. Clear everything from Settings, any time. No remote copy exists.
